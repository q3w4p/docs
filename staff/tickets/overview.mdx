<Note>
  Tickets are the primary way members contact staff privately. Treat every ticket seriously — members using them are trusting you to help.
</Note>

## Types of Tickets

edates has two ticket types:

| Type | Purpose |
| --- | --- |
| **Report** | A member reporting another user for rule violations |
| **Support** | A member needing help with something (appeals, questions, issues) |

---

## Handling Report Tickets

Report tickets are opened when a member wants to flag someone for breaking the rules.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Greet and acknowledge">
    When you claim the ticket, greet the member and let them know you're handling it. Keep it simple:

    > "Hey, I've got your ticket. Go ahead and share what happened."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Gather the details">
    Ask for (or check for) the following:

    - Who are they reporting? (username \+ ID if possible)
    - What happened? (description of the offense)
    - When did it happen?
    - Do they have evidence? (screenshots, video, message links)
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the evidence">
    Don't take reports at face value. Check the accused member's history with `,history @user` and review any evidence provided. If something seems off or exaggerated, dig deeper before acting.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Take action">
    If the report is valid, take the appropriate action per the [Punishment Guide](/staff/moderation/punishments). If it requires a ban or senior review, escalate via the [Escalation Policy](/staff/policies/escalation).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close the ticket">
    Let the reporter know what action was taken (without sharing confidential details). Thank them for reporting and close the ticket.

    > "Thanks for the report we've reviewed it and taken appropriate action. Let us know if anything else comes up."
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Handling Support Tickets

Support tickets cover appeals, questions, or anything that doesn't fit in a report.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Greet and identify the issue">
    Acknowledge the ticket and find out what they need:

    > "Hey! What can I help you with today?"
  </Step>
  <Step title="Resolve or escalate">
    Handle what you can at your rank level. If it's a ban appeal or something that requires Admin review, escalate it to the right person and let the member know you're doing so.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Close when resolved">
    Once the issue is addressed, close the ticket and let them know it's been handled.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Common Scenarios

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Someone reports a member for harassment">
    Greet them, ask for screenshots and the reported user's tag/ID. Check the accused's history. If harassment is confirmed, issue the appropriate mute or warn. If it's severe (targeted, ongoing, involves personal info), escalate to Admin\+.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Someone appeals their ban">
    This is a support ticket. Do **not** unban yourself — pass it to Senior Mod or Admin to review. Let the member know their appeal is being reviewed and that you'll follow up.

    > "Thanks for reaching out. I'll pass this to a Senior Mod for review — we'll get back to you."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Someone reports another member for doxxing">
    Immediately escalate to Admin\+. Jail the accused if they're still in the server. Do not discuss the specifics of the case with the reporter beyond confirming you're handling it.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Someone opens a ticket with no message">
    Give them a moment, then prompt them:

    > "Hey Go ahead and let us know what you need help with."

    If they don't respond after a reasonable wait e..g 1 hour, or just leave the ticket open. mute them for 5 hours with reason:

    > ",mute @user 5h Don't make tickets and dont respond or say anything."
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Someone is rude in the ticket">
    Stay professional. You don't need to tolerate being berated — if it continues after a reminder, close the ticket and note it:

    > "I'm happy to help, but I'll need you to keep things civil. If that continues, I'll have to close this ticket."
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## General Ticket Rules

- **Don't leave tickets hanging.** If you claimed it, handle it or pass it off.
- **One staff member per ticket.** Don't pile in unless asked.
- **Don't share what happens in tickets** with members outside the ticket.
- **Always be professional** — tickets are often the member's only direct interaction with staff.
- If a ticket is outside your rank's ability to resolve, **escalate immediately** rather than stalling.

---

<Warning>
  Never close a ticket without at least acknowledging the member. An unacknowledged ticket feels like being ignored — that reflects badly on the whole staff team.
</Warning>
