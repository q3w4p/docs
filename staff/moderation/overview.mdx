## The Moderation Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check History">
    Before doing anything, run `,history @user`. Knowing their track record determines what punishment level they're at.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assess the Situation">
    Is this a first offense or a pattern? Minor or severe? Is de-escalation an option before a formal action?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Act with a Reason">
    Issue the appropriate punishment via bleed. Always include a specific reason — not just "rule break."
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log It">
    bleed auto-logs to #mod-log. For verbal warnings or complex situations, also post in the staff channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Escalate if Needed">
    If the situation is beyond your permissions or unclear, ping Senior Staff or Admin. See the [Escalation Guide](/staff/policies/escalation).
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Verbal Warnings vs. Formal Actions

Not every situation requires a bot command. For minor, first-time offenses, a verbal warning in chat is enough:

> "Hey @user, keep things on topic — this is your warning."

If the behavior continues after a verbal warning, use `,warn`. Verbal warnings are **not logged by the bot** — post a note in the staff channel if it feels relevant.

---

## Handling Beef / Arguments

Don't warn for regular beef unless it escalates. Your job is to **de-escalate first** — step in, cool the situation down. If it goes too far or drags on:

- Issue 10-minute mutes to both parties, or just the person who keeps pushing it if the other has dropped it.

---

## Leniency Guidelines

Some rules allow for leniency depending on context:

- **Sexual convos** — you may be lenient on conversations that aren't making others uncomfortable and aren't going too far. Use judgment.
- **Talking about k1rk** — you do not need to punish talking about it. Only mute if they post an image, or go too far.
- **DM leaks** — technically allowed if both parties are fine with it and it doesn't violate ToS. Face leaks are not considered a leak if the person already posted that picture in the server themselves.

<Note>
  When in doubt on a leniency call, err on the side of a verbal warning and ask a Senior Mod or Admin for a second opinion.
</Note>
