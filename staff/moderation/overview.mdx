## The Moderation Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Check History">
    Before doing anything, run `,history @user`. Knowing their track record determines what punishment level they're at. A first offense and a fifth offense for the same thing are handled very differently.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assess the Situation">
    Is this a first offense or a pattern? Minor or severe? Is de-escalation an option? Not every situation needs a bot command — read the room first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Act With a Reason">
    Issue the appropriate punishment via bleed. Always include a specific reason. Example: `,mute @user 10m Repeated hard-r bypass after prior warn` — not just `,mute @user bad`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Log It">
    bleed auto-logs to #log. For **complex** situations (e.g. ongoing beef, potential ban, or anything involving a minor), also post a note in the staff channel.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Escalate if Needed">
    If the situation is beyond your permissions or unclear, ping Senior Staff or Admin. See the [Escalation Guide](/staff/policies/escalation). Don't sit on it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Verbal Warnings vs. Formal Actions

Not every situation requires a bot command. For minor, first-time offenses, a verbal warning in chat is fine:

> "Hey @user, stop unneccessary beef."

If the behavior continues after a verbal warning, escalate to `,warn`. Verbal warnings **are not logged by the bot** — if it feels relevant, drop a note in the staff channel.

## Real Scenarios

<AccordionGroup>
  <Accordion title="Member spamming rapidly in gen chat">
    Check history — first offense? Issue a verbal warning: _"Hey! please stop spamming its annoying!!"_ If it continues, `,warn @user Spam in chat after verbal warning`. Next offense: `,mute @user 5m Continued spam`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Two members beefing in chat">
    Don't warn straight away. Step in: _"Hey both of you, drop it."_ If one keeps going after the other has stopped: `,mute @instigator 10m Continued argument after warning`. If both keep going: mute both 10 minutes.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Someone posts an image that might be soft NSFW">
    Check if it's borderline or clear. Borderline? Verbal warn and delete the image. Clear soft NSFW? `,warn @user Soft NSFW` and delete. If they keep posting: `,mute @user 10m Repeated soft NSFW after warning`.
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Member posts a gore image">
    No warning needed. `,ban @user Gore` immediately. If you don't have ban perms: `,jail @user Gore — ban pending` 
  </Accordion>

  <Accordion title="Someone uses the n-word or a bypass attempt">
    `,mute @user 5m Hard-r bypass`. No prior warning needed this is a direct mute offense. Each repeat adds 5 minutes. Resets daily.
  </Accordion>
</AccordionGroup>

## Leniency Guidelines

Some rules allow for leniency depending on context:

- **Sexual convos** — you may be lenient if no one is uncomfortable and it isn't going too far. Use judgment.
- **Talking about kirk** — you do not need to punish talk about it. Only mute if they post an image or go too far.
- **DM leaks** — technically allowed if both parties are fine with it and it doesn't violate ToS. Face leaks are not considered a leak if the person already posted that picture in the server themselves.

<Note>
  When in doubt on a leniency call, err on the side of a verbal warning and ask a Senior Mod or Admin for a second opinion.
</Note>
