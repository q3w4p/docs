## When to Escalate

Some situations are beyond your rank's permissions or require senior judgment. **Always escalate** when:

- You don't have ban perms but a ban is clearly needed — jail the member and ping someone with ban perms
- The offense involves **doxxing, sexual content leaks, or false allegations** — jail and ping Senior\+
- You suspect a **coordinated raid** — ping a Manager immediately
- A situation involves **a minor** in an inappropriate context
- You're unsure if a permanent ban is warranted
- The offending member is **another staff member**
- You have a **personal conflict of interest** with the member involved

---

## How to Escalate

<Steps>
  <Step title="Take Immediate Action">
    Don't wait — jail, mute, or lock the channel if the situation requires it. Protect the server first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Ping the Right Rank">
    Ping `@Senior Moderator` or `@Admin` depending on what's needed. See the table below.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Don't Discuss It Publicly">
    Do not discuss the case in public channels or with the member involved until a Senior Mod or Admin has reviewed it.
  </Step>
</Steps>

---


## Rank-Specific Escalation

| Situation | Escalate To |
| --- | --- |
| Need a ban issued | Any staff with ban perms |
| Complex jail case | Senior Moderator\+ |
| Doxxing, sexual leaks, false allegations | Admin\+ |
| Raid in progress | Manager (immediately) |
| Another staff member breaking rules | HOS or Manager\+ |

---

<Warning>
  Do not sit on an escalation. If it needs to go up the chain, do it immediately — waiting makes situations harder to resolve and can put the server at risk.
</Warning>
