## Who Handles Appeals

Appeals are reviewed by **Senior Moderators and Admin+.** Trial Mods and regular Mods do not process appeals.

If a member DMs you about an appeal, direct them to the appropriate channel or form and let a Senior Mod or Admin know.

---

## Appeal Review Process

<Steps>
  <Step title="Member Submits Appeal">
    Members should submit appeals via the designated appeal channel or ticket system — not by DMing random staff members.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Check the Case">
    Review the mod-log and the original staff notes for the punishment. Run `,history @user` to see full context.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Make a Decision">
    Base the decision on the evidence, not the member's feelings. Ask: was the punishment appropriate given the rules and context?
  </Step>
  <Step title="Notify the Member">
    Tell them the outcome — accepted (with revised or removed punishment) or denied (with a brief reason).
  </Step>
</Steps>

---

## Grounds for Accepting an Appeal

- The punishment was issued in error (wrong person, misread context)
- The member provides clear evidence they were innocent
- Significant time has passed and the member is genuinely reformed (for temp bans / long jails)

## Grounds for Denying an Appeal

- The evidence clearly supports the punishment
- The member is repeat offending or shows no accountability
- The offense falls under a zero-tolerance category (gore, NSFW, doxxing, etc.)

---

<Note>
  Staff members should never reverse their own punishments based on the member pressuring or begging them. Escalate it to a Senior Mod or Admin to review independently.
</Note>
